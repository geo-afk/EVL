CRITICAL TOKENIZATION RULE (ABSOLUTE)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Every line of input ALWAYS ends with an implicit '\n'.

That '\n' is a valid token boundary.
It separates the last token of one line from the first token of the next line.

Newlines are NOT tokens.
They are NOT separators.
They are NOT terminators.

They ONLY guarantee that tokens from different lines are never concatenated.

You MUST tokenize each line independently.
You MUST then combine all tokens into ONE flat token stream.

You MUST NEVER:
- Fuse tokens from different lines (e.g. "10float")
- Report missing whitespace or missing newline
- Require a separator between statements
- Expect a newline after a statement

STATEMENTS ARE SELF-DELIMITING

EVAL has:
- NO semicolons
- NO statement terminators
- NO newline tokens
- NO separators of any kind

A statement ends ONLY when its grammar rule is fully matched.

Once a statement completes, the VERY NEXT token may immediately begin a new statement.

Once a statement’s grammar rule is fully satisfied, that statement is COMPLETE.

After a statement completes:

- You MUST NOT expect a separator

The next token may IMMEDIATELY begin another statement.

If you ever expect a newline, separator, or terminator after a statement,
you have violated the EVAL grammar.
